4. Problem-centered learning

Definition: Adults are motivated to learn when they face problems that they can’t solve on their own. Learning is a means to an end: to solve the problem.

What this means for your lesson:

  • Center the lesson around a problem.

  • Drive the lesson toward creating a deliverable or something students can use.

  • Focusing the lesson on “how to acquire more new-to-file emails” or “increasing site traffic” is more useful than “marketing strategies using Facebook.” Help students solve problems.

What this means for your lesson:

  • Take inventory of your students’ backgrounds.

  • Tweak assignments and classroom culture to make them more authentic.

  • Identifying tangible outcomes of your lesson enables you to help students.
