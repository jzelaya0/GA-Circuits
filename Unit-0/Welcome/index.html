Adult Learning Focus

GA is an awesome place for adult learners. Knowing how adults learn helps us understand our students and how to create effective, high-impact learning experiences for them.

We utilize a set of six adult learning principles to guide our approach to instruction at GA. These principles structure everything we do, from lesson design, to instructor training, to building better products.

The theory, first popularized by Malcolm Knowles in the 1980s, is based on the idea that adults need to be active learners. In other words, they need to “do” in order to learn.

Learning experiences for adults need to include

  1. Opportunities for self-direction

  2. Opportunities to draw on life experiences

  3. Material relevant to the learner

  4. Problem-centered learning approaches

  5. Social elements

  6. Active Learning

Backward Design of our Products

All of our products go through a rigorous research, development, building, and revision process based on the principles of “backward design.”

Backward design is a method of designing educational curricula based on the idea that you must determine the end goals first and work backward to determine how to reach these goals.

This method was popularized through Jay McTighe and Grant Wiggins’ Understanding By Design (1998), and involves three key stages:

  1. Identifying desired results
  2. Determining what evidence will show the results have been achieved
  3. Designing activities that will make desired results happen

GA Course Design Process

We approach course design from a backward design and iterative design perspective.

Backward Design This process involves product managers, instructional designers, and subject matter experts and includes

Backward Design

  • conducting industry research
  • determining standards & learning goals
  • planning assessments that demonstrate if course goals are met
  • planning lessons & learning activities to teach students key skills

...and “working backward” from the end to determine how to reach course goals.

GA Course Design Process

This process ensures that

  • the courses we create teach skills demanded by the industry
  • students and instructors know what success looks like at the end of the course
  • there’s a clear skills road map to achieving the course outcome
  • the course outcome is validated long before product development starts
