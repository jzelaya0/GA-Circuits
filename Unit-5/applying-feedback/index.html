Struggling Student - Academic

Definition

Instructor(s) have identified an issue from one or more assessments or assignments.

Struggling Student - Academic

Tactics

Identify it early and discuss it often! Although it’s not comfortable, it’s better to face it and start working on a solution.

It’s a progression. Level of involvement and documentation increases throughout. Ensure there is some documentation from the beginning.

Ask yourself - what does this situation need from me at this point? (information gathering is most likely first).

  1. Identify/clarify issue.
  2. Determine topics/questions to address. Use specific specific examples/behaviors.
  3. Discuss corrective measures.
  4. Determine next steps for student action plan.

Struggling Student - Academic

Key Points to Keep in Mind

Use a gradual approach. Keep on top of and address small issues as they arise so that students know there’s accountability and a tight feedback loop.

Not sure how to start? Even a casual comment to start a conversation about class work can go a long way. Then you can follow up with the student at a later time.

“It feels much worse to wait..then it’s week 8 and you’ve hoped that students will improve, but then it’s too late when they haven’t and you don’t have any solutions to recommend for them to get back on track.”- Matt S

“Hope is not the solution here. It’s a necessary component, but not THEE solution.” - Matt Scilipoti

Note that you’ll practice Gradual release of responsibility as an instructor. First, you lead with interventions and are 100% responsible for flagging student progress. Over time in the course, your goal is to gradually led the student lead these conversations.

For reference. Timeline & Minimum Requirements for Tracking Student Progress

Struggling Student - Academic

  • Scenario

  • Solution

It’s week 2 of your course. A student has not successfully completed any of the homework, and you’re just realizing that now. Somehow the student slipped through the cracks of homework review because they submitted something each time. What do you do?

Struggling Student - Academic

  • Scenario

  • Solution

Do not delay. Understand that you are late. It’s the instructor’s responsibility to lead this conversation. (Note: later in the course, students own more of this conversation, but in week 2 you are setting the precedent.)

Correction should have started on the first missed homework

Ask yourself - what does this situation need from me at this point?

This situation needs information gathering.

Setup a meeting with the student ASAP. You are, most likely, gathering information to process with other instructors and your producer. Ask questions to understand the issue first (is it knowledge, process, personal)? Follow process [LINK] outlined above.

If problem is identified and solvable, sit with the student to see it solved, together. Now. (Don’t just identify the problem for them & leave -- solve it with them in the moment).

Struggling Student - Personal

Definition

Student’s demeanor, routine, and/or behavior is cause for concern and/or affecting his/her GA experience, though not necessarily academically. i.e. Student has been tardy regularly , has just surpassed maximum tardy guideline, but is in good academic standing.

Struggling Student - Personal

Tactics

If situation continues, or is serious enough, Producer should be included in any follow-up meetings.Producer handles discussion of sentencing. Above topics are discussed, with greater clarity on ramifications and seriousness. Verbal agreements are replaced by written agreements.

  1. Determine cause via 1:1 student conversation, working to have student identify the issue. (Note - can be as simple as asking a student how they are doing or why X has been happening in an informal conversation.)
  2. Inform producer of behavior, since behavior relates or could relate to the GA contract. Producer takes lead on any contractual issues.
  3. Discuss ramifications of GA contract and options available at our discretion.
  4. Discuss basic expectations and respect for others in relation to their issue.
  5. Discuss/clarify priorities. i.e. Proper Sleep over Completing all homework.
  6. Create Action Plan with Student. For lesser offenses, instructor leads conversation and gives verbal action plan. For more serious offenses, Producer should lead the conversation and leave with a written action plan.

Struggling Student - Personal

Key Points to Keep in Mind

Approach with empathy. You never know what a student might be going through.

Level of seriousness and level of specificity increases with repeated instances and/or meetings with GA staff. Time and level of offense control level of escalation.

Be active about identifying issues / trends early.

Eventually, all personal issues will lead to academic issues (varying with intensity and frequency). So, it’s important to discuss early.

Class priorities follow the mantra of the Agile Manifesto. i.e. Proper Sleep over Completing all homework “while there is value in the items on the right, we value the items on the left more.”

Ensure you approach with same seriousness and empathy as academic issue.

Struggling Student - Personal

  • Scenario

  • Solution

A student in your class has been tardy 2 times and seems disengaged in class. His academic record is current unaffected but he’s unmotivated and does not participate.

What is your first step? What is plan of action?

Struggling Student - Personal

  • Scenario

  • Solution

Our job is to find the cause of the disengagement. Tardiness is most likely an indicator.

  1. Discuss with one or more instructors to see if they have seen similar indicators.
  2. Have 1:1 with student indicating that we’ve noticed that the student’s tardiness is approaching the GA contract limit (i.e. 3 tardies = absence). Discuss ramifications.
  3. Try to identify cause without crossing personal boundaries. Offer if you can help the student in any way.
  4. Reiterate that everyone at GA is supportive and is available to discuss or assist . Address any misconceptions that the ‘problem is too small’ for GA attention. Reiterate that GA is happy and ready to help with any level of problem that affects GA class / experience.

Code of Conduct - Potential Breach

Definition

A single occurrence of a breach of Code Of Conduct is grounds for immediate action. Refer to Code of Conduct for actions and penalty.

Code of Conduct - Potential Breach

Tactics

  1. Inform the other instructors and Producer.
  2. Ensure everyone has reviewed the specific details in the Code of Conduct, related to this breach.
  3. Discuss ramifications of GA contract and options available at our discretion.
  4. Set up 1:1 ASAP (Instructor, Producer, and student) and have this document handy during the discussion.
  5. Determine cause via 1:1 student conversation, working to have student identify the issue. (Note - can be as simple as asking a student how they are doing or why X has been happening in an informal conversation.)
  6. If applicable, clearly identify the student’s action or behavior as violating the Code of Conduct.
  7. Discuss basic expectations and respect for others in relation to their issue.
  8. Discuss ramifications of GA contract and options available at our discretion.
  9. Create Action Plan with Student. Producer leads conversation and gives written action plan.
  10. Work with Producer to prepare apology and statement for affected students and/or class. Strongly suggest that student does the same.

Code of Conduct - Potential Breach

Key Points to Keep in Mind

Try to assume the best of the student and that it was an honest mistake.

If you are uncomfortable in the situation or when learning of the situation, students will be, too.

Practice zero tolerance. If a student or instructor is making others uncomfortable, it affects the students’ learning, the class’s demeanor, and the student experience negatively.

When in doubt, flag up to producer!

If you are present during the incident, immediately address the student by saying that “that behavior is not appropriate for our class” and follow up with him or her at the next break.

Code of Conduct - Potential Breach

  • Scenario

  • Solution

Student uses racial slur in front of class during a presentation.

Code of Conduct - Potential Breach

  • Scenario

  • Solution

  1. Immediately indicate that “that behavior is NOT appropriate for this class.” Do not stop class. You’ll have the opportunity to address the misconduct later, but it’s important keep the class going while condemning the behavior in the moment.
  2. Take issue to other Instructors and producers as soon as possible.
  3. Schedule 1:1. with student, Producer, and Instructor.

Advanced Student

Definition

Student who is a quick learner, has prior experience, or otherwise is ahead of others in class and bored, disengaged, and/or disruptive.

Advanced Student

Tactics

  1. Discuss during regular 1:1.
  2. Give suggestions that push student. These include using the course as an opportunity to
    • Polish skills
    • Work with others
    • Present to class
    • Maximize reaching goals
    • Choose extra project to support (open source or GA-related)
  3. Direct Mindset.
    • Set expectations and acknowledge that everyone learns at a different pace. Encourage growth mindset by asking student “what can you clean out of this course?”
  4. Encourage student to make an action plan and follow up with you on it.

Advanced Student

Key Points to Keep in Mind

We are fighting boredom and expectations here. Immersive courses are not designed to help a student achieve mastery in a subject. The course will introduce students to the concepts and set them up for continual learning. It is up to them to find a way to stretch themselves.

Practicing differentiation and encouraging students to support each other in class helps stave off this student behavior and need for this kind of feedback.

Be cautious about separating the student from the class or putting them on a pedestal.

Advanced Student

  • Scenario

  • Solution

Student comes in with prior experience in coding and is bored by the second week. He’s starting to disengage in class and just surfS the web for ‘new stuff’.

Advanced Student

  • Scenario

  • Solution

  1. During your next 1:1, ask the student what excites him.
  2. Suggest a few simple ways that the student can bring those interests into daily work or level up weekly labs or projects.
  3. Encourage student to see class time as growth opportunity - can help support others and or extend work he’s already doing.
